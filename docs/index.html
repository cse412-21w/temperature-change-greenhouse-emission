Introduction

One of the most important issues the world and all world citizens face is the climate change in this technological era. The record of historical temperature change provides us the chance to show how it progresses over times at various time periods in different countries and also overall world temperature change. Carbon emission is one of the main causes of climate change that is worth more attention, so we want to show people the current situation to raise awareness and call for actions for possible reduction in carbon footage so that we can contribute as much as we can to save the world. Greenhouse Gas Emission Proportions in Top Countries

Greenhouse gas is mainly composed by Carbon Dioxide, Methane, and Nitrous Oxide. More information can be checked in this Wikipedia page. Within this 20 years, the proportion of carbon dioxide increases among those countries, whereas that of methane decreases.

From the dataset between 1990 and 2018, even though there are some missing country data, United States has the highest greenhouse gas emission in every year. Throughout this 20-year span, alomost every country increases the gas emission, whereas a few countries such as Germany and United Kingdom have a downward trend.

British parliament passed Climate Change Act in 2008, and the United Kingdom became the first country in the world that published legislation to handle climate change. Recently, more and more countries have paid increasing attention on greenhouse gas emission and climate change, especially solving carbon emission.

Emission Of Greenhouse Gases

Carbon Dioxide, methane, and Nitrous oxide, are three major components for greenhouse gases. And they are the one of the significant causes of the global warming. Following graphs display the flunctuation of three gases during 1990 to 2018. We can observe that these three gases all have an incresing trend around year 2017.

How much does the world temperature change from year to year

The overall Trend is that the temperature steadily increases from year to year. Starting from 1997, there is no longer a flunctuation on temperature changes, and temperature is higher by about 2 degree celcius compared to the baseline temperature.

Insight

Human-induced climate change has contributed to changing patterns of extreme weather across the globe, from longer and hotter heat waves to heavier rains. While we were developing a more well-rounded understanding about how greenhouse gases affect global warming, it is notable that greenhouse gas emissions, mainly carbon dioxide and methane, continue to increase and the gap between current trends and agreed climate targets has widened. Through this project, we hope to remind people that greenhouse gases from human activities are the most significant driver of observed climate change since the mid-20th century and as greenhouse gas emissions from human activities increase, they build up in the atmosphere and warm the climate, leading to many other changes around the world—in the atmosphere, on land, and in the oceans. These changes can have negative effects on people, society, and the environment—including plants and animals and these changes can last for a very long period.
