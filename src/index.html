Temperature Change and Greenhouse Emission Over Years

One of the most important issues the world and all world citizens face is the climate change in this technological era. The record of historical temperature change provides us the chance to show how it progresses over times at various time periods in different countries and also overall world temperature change. Carbon emission is one of the main causes of climate change that is worth more attention, so we want to show people the current situation to raise awareness and call for actions for possible reduction in carbon footage so that we can contribute as much as we can to save the world. We plan to use global maps to present an overall distribution of both greenhouse gas emissions and temperatures. By choosing two years with a long-time span, we can see which countries have the most greenhouse gas emissions. We intend to use an interactive line to present the greenhouse emission and temperatures of countries around the world. From this, we may be able to display the relationship between greenhouse emission and temperature changes. We will present the meterological year temeprature change over years by a bar chart. Also, we would like to do deep research on the reasons behind some noticeable changes and present the reasons to the audience. More.

The following interactive graph shows the average emission of carbon dioxide from year 1990 to 2018, and the changes of temperature for each year. By using the selection bar, one could simply see the average emission of carbon dioxide and temperature changes of a particular country. It is notable that most of countries experienced a growth in temperature during these year.

How much does the world temperature change from year to year

It is getting hotter! The bar chart shows how much is the meterological temperature change from baseline between 1962 and 2018. Negative number means that this year's temperature is lower than the baseline temperature, and positive number means higher temperature The overall trend is that the temperature steadily increases from year to year. Starting from 1997, there is no longer a flunctuation on temperature changes, and temperature is higher by about 2 degree celcius compared to the baseline temperature.
